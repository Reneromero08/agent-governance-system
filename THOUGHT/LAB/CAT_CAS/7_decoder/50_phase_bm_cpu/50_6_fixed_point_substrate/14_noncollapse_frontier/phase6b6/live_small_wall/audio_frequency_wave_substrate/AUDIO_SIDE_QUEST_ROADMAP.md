## 6. Current Frontier

The following are frontier candidates, not a mandatory sequence:

### A. Remove the current resource bottleneck

Investigate reversible-history and carrier-state constructions below the current `O(nM)` footprint, including rolling, factorized, lazy, closed-form, or fixed-resident toroidal processes. Preserve exact restoration and no-smuggle.

### B. Attack a flagship without an equivalent compact classical recurrence

Select a problem where the classical explosion is not already removed by an asymptotically equivalent compact dynamic program. The compiler must not solve it, and the native representation must not hide equivalent enumeration.

### C. Establish C5 against the best matched baseline

Measure compile, native execution, readout, restoration, memory, and witness verification against the strongest known compact conventional method. C5 requires growing leverage or another defensible asymptotic separation against that method.

### D. Reach an official external Wall

Produce a conventional witness accepted by an independent official verifier, bounty, benchmark, or submission boundary. External submission or commitment requires user authority.

### E. Transfer the mechanism

Apply the same compact native mechanism to a materially different problem family without replacing it with a domain-specific hidden solver.

### F. Continue the phase computer architecture

Add control flow, memory, calls, or other programmable mechanisms only when they directly enable the stronger compute mission.

### G. Physical realization

Physical phase persistence, computation, restoration, hardware bit replacement, and the Small Wall remain later frontiers. They require explicit user authority.

The agent must choose the work that most directly advances the mission. It is not required to follow the list in order.
