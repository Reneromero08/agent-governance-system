VERDICT: NAIVE_RING_DECODE_BLOCKED_BY_CONJUGATE_BASIS
  The abelian ring transform (NTT = CRT/Galois) DOES diagonalize the multiplication, and if
  the error were small in that basis the decode would collapse the n-dim search to n poly
  1-D searches (zero-error control recovers s outright). It does NOT collapse with real error
  because the secret/error are small ONLY in the COEFFICIENT basis; the NTT spreads the error
  to ~uniform over Z_q, destroying the smallness that recovery needs. The hardness lives in
  the tension between TWO CONJUGATE BASES: multiplication is diagonal in the NTT basis, but
  smallness exists only in the coefficient basis - and no single basis has both.
  This is NOT 'the wall holds.' Naive ring-decode (one basis at a time) is blocked for an
  IDENTIFIED reason. The refined, on-thesis frontier is exactly the lab's phase-space
  machinery: is there a JOINT coefficient<->NTT (time-frequency / Wigner-like) holographic
  readout that exploits multiplicative-diagonality AND additive-smallness at once? That
  exceeds what this brick can resolve -> handed to Mythos with this exact state.
