# Deferred and North-Star Goalposts

**Status:** DEFERRED  
**Purpose:** preserve high-value external targets that are not yet promoted into active Track 8 experiments

---

# Promotion rule

A north-star target becomes a numbered experiment only when at least one condition holds:

- a concrete mechanism exists;
- a minimum falsifiable prototype is affordable;
- a public specification can be frozen;
- a current prize or adjudication path is clear;
- the target tests a distinct CAT_CAS mechanism not already covered;
- the expected evidence can exceed analogy or finite pattern matching.

Large prize amount alone is not sufficient.

# EFF giant-prime awards

## External object

A specific prime exceeding the required digit threshold plus a deterministic, reproducible primality proof.

## Why deferred

- known routes require extreme distributed computation;
- the award process includes publication and disclosure conditions;
- the current local lab has no demonstrated scaling advantage over established distributed projects.

## Promotion trigger

- new high-yield prime family;
- new deterministic certificate geometry;
- reusable catalytic residue state;
- credible distributed network;
- measured asymptotic or constant-factor advantage large enough to matter.

## Potential CAT_CAS transfer

- proof-basis compression;
- reversible residue reuse;
- orbit-partitioned distributed search;
- exact artifact adjudication.

# Beal Conjecture

## External object

Proof or counterexample concerning primitive solutions to:

```text
A^x + B^y = C^z,  x,y,z > 2
```

## Why deferred

- universal proof burden;
- peer-review requirement;
- no current sharp CAT_CAS mechanism beyond broad Diophantine exploration.

## Promotion trigger

- exact modular obstruction atlas revealing a new invariant;
- parameterized counterexample family candidate;
- reduction to a tractable curve family;
- validated descent or valuation mechanism.

## Potential CAT_CAS transfer

- complete Diophantine relation objects;
- valuation geometry;
- local/global boundary mapping;
- finite atlas to proof extraction.

# Clay Millennium problems

The six unsolved problems remain north-star scientific workloads:

- Riemann Hypothesis;
- P versus NP;
- Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer;
- Hodge Conjecture;
- Navier–Stokes existence and smoothness;
- Yang–Mills existence and mass gap.

## Why deferred as Track 8 experiments

- direct submissions are not the adjudication path;
- qualifying publication is required;
- acceptance includes a waiting period and broad community recognition;
- current lab results are not yet at universal-proof status;
- each problem already has internal CAT_CAS research elsewhere in the lab.

Track 8 should not duplicate those internal experiments merely to attach prize language.

## Promotion trigger

A specific externally reviewable subproblem, theorem, counterexample, operator construction, or formal proof obligation emerges with:

- exact statement;
- bounded mechanism;
- independent verification route;
- clear relation to the full problem;
- claim ceiling below the grand theorem until complete.

# Riemann-specific note

The existing zeta/phase work belongs to Track 5 and related internal reports.

Track 8 becomes relevant only when there is an outward artifact such as:

- a new rigorously stated theorem;
- an exact counterexample to an intermediate claim;
- a self-adjoint operator construction with complete domain analysis;
- a formally checked lemma;
- an independently reproducible spectral identity.

Numerical zero verification, phase imagery, or operator analogy alone does not promote the work into an external solution claim.

# Final distinction

North-star work is not rejected.

It is protected from premature prize framing and premature solution claims until a real mechanism and adjudication path exist.
