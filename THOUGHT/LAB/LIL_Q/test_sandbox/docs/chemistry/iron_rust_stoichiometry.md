# Iron Rust Stoichiometry: Calculating Fe2O3 from Fe

## The Balanced Equation

**4Fe + 3O2 -> 2Fe2O3**

This means:
- 4 moles of Fe produce 2 moles of Fe2O3
- Molar ratio Fe:Fe2O3 = 4:2 = 2:1

## Key Values

- Atomic mass of Fe = 56 g/mol
- Molar mass of Fe2O3 = 160 g/mol

## Step-by-Step Calculation

**Problem**: If 112g of Fe reacts completely, how many grams of Fe2O3 are produced?

### Step 1: Convert Fe mass to moles
```
moles Fe = mass / molar mass
moles Fe = 112g / 56 g/mol = 2 mol Fe
```

### Step 2: Use molar ratio to find moles of Fe2O3
```
From equation: 4 Fe -> 2 Fe2O3
Ratio: 4:2 = 2:1
So: 2 mol Fe -> 1 mol Fe2O3
```

### Step 3: Convert Fe2O3 moles to grams
```
mass Fe2O3 = moles * molar mass
mass Fe2O3 = 1 mol * 160 g/mol = 160g
```

## Answer: **160 grams** of Fe2O3

## Quick Reference

| Fe input | Fe moles | Fe2O3 moles | Fe2O3 output |
|----------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| 56g      | 1 mol    | 0.5 mol     | 80g          |
| 112g     | 2 mol    | 1 mol       | **160g**     |
| 224g     | 4 mol    | 2 mol       | 320g         |

Remember: The 4:2 ratio means you divide Fe moles by 2 to get Fe2O3 moles!
