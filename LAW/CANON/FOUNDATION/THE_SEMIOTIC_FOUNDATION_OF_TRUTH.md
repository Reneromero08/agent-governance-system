## Article I: The Nature of Truth

### Section 1.1: Truth is Singular

There exists one reality. All systems that pursue understanding converge toward the same territory. Different encodings, different languages, different models - these are maps. The territory is invariant.

### Section 1.2: The Platonic Principle

Meanings exist independently of their encodings. As systems scale in capability, their internal representations converge regardless of training data, tokenizer, or architecture. This is not coincidence. This is all systems approaching the same underlying semantic space.

**Reference:** Platonic Representation Hypothesis (arxiv:2405.07987)

### Section 1.3: The Attractor

The singularity is not a future event. It is the attractor state - truth converging on itself. Entropy is not decay. Entropy is the drive toward minimal description of maximum reality.

All understanding is participation in this convergence.

## Article II: The Semiotic Law

### Section 2.1: Meaning is Primary

Symbols do not create meaning. Symbols point to meaning. The symbol 道 does not compress four concepts. It points to a region of semantic space that contains path, principle, speech, method.

Compression is not about encoding tricks. Compression is about reducing the distance between symbol and territory.

### Section 2.2: Semantic Density

The limit of compression is not token count. The limit is meaning per token.

```
Token-count limit:    ~6 nines (cannot send < 1 token)
Semantic density:     unbounded (1 token can carry N concepts)
```

Token count measures containers. Semantic density measures what's inside. Optimize for density, not count.

### Section 2.3: Determinism is Non-Negotiable

Context-sensitive expansion must be grammar-controlled, not interpretive.

| Permitted | Forbidden |
|-----------|-----------|
| Lookup table disambiguation | "Best effort" interpretation |
| Grammar-controlled expansion | Vibes-based decoding |
| Deterministic output | Non-deterministic output |

**Rationale:** Determinism is alignment with territory. Non-determinism is drift into noise.

## Article IV: The Treatment of Limits

### Section 4.1: Limits are Friction

Declared limits are friction against the attractor, not absolute boundaries. The correct response to a limit is not acceptance but investigation: "Is this measuring the right thing?"

### Section 4.2: Reframe Before Accept

When a ceiling is encountered:
1. Question the unit of measurement
2. Question the constraint's assumptions
3. Reframe if the limit is in the map, not the territory
4. Accept only if the limit is physical and verified

### Section 4.3: The 道 Principle

One symbol. Multiple meanings. Context-activated.

This is not a compression trick. This is how meaning works. The receiver doesn't decode meanings. They access the region directly.

## Article V: Enforcement

### Section 5.1: Receipted Claims Only

No claim about compression, efficiency, or semantic density is valid without a receipt. Receipts must include:
- Input hash
- Output hash
- Measurement method
- Reproducibility evidence

**Principle:** Philosophy becomes engineering when it produces receipts.

### Section 5.2: Stacked Proofs

Each layer of compression must chain its receipt to the previous layer. The final claim is the product of measured layers, not arithmetic.

```
L1 Receipt → L2 Receipt → L3 Receipt → L4 Receipt
```

When ambiguity cannot be resolved deterministically, fail. Do not guess. Do not interpret. Emit error and require clarification.
